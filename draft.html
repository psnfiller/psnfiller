We didn't do every much on Monday - we had hopes of going to the muesum, but it was closed. The Muesum would defy all attemts to visit it. We did do a pile of planning and sorting stuff out, as well as tromping up the local hill. On tuesday, we jumped in a hire car and took off on a road trip.

We drove the southern toursit loop from Salta - down to cafalate, up a smaller road to cachi, then though los .... national park back to Salta. The lady in the tuorist infomation office assured us we could do this in two days, sadly, this was not the case.

the road down to Cafa is kinda boring until we got into the desert in .... Once again, weird and wonderful rock foramtions drawn b y the wind and water. It was a lovely fast drive, though the Salta traffic is insane.

Cafa is a lovely place. its the second town in argentina for wine, and if you are looking for a place to drink wine, go there rather than mendroza. The local wine even comes in Ice cream form!. We wandered around for a while, drunk some of the local wine (torrentes - its a lovely white that people say is hard to get outside argentina. ate embedapdasa while drinking wine from a jug, and peeked at the local museum - tons of pottery recovered from local arglogical digs. we were a bit pushed for time, so we couldn't stay a day, much as we wanted to.

the drive to Cachi was mostly on dirt roads. we set out zooming along on tarmac, though wine yards, looking at wonderful mountings with the sun blazing down. we overtook a local in his junker (one of the things that got me when i moved to Ireland is that one sees more cars with dented paneling etc that ...). we promptly hitthe dirt road, and crawled along at 30 k. the local promptly overtook us, going much the same speed as he was doing on tarmac. huh.

The scenery on the drive is fantasic. look at the pictures!

We saw parrots, woodpeckers, a small kestrel and a few Caracaras.

we got a flat about 20k from cachi. we had been shaken about so much we didn't hear it go, but a passerby tooted to let us know. we jumped out, jacked the car up, and promptly failed to loosen the nuts. No amount of stomping, standing, and brute force would move the nuts. We asked a local for help - he did the same stomping routine. He asked a friend, who had a extender for the wrench. the extra metre of leverage did the trick. science! The nuts had probably expanded in the heat.

Since we were now without a spare tire, had 100s of k on dirt roads to go, and it was getting late, we stopped in Cachi. Phoned up Hertz, who said they would deliver a spare in a hour or two. The guy who was assigned to .... we spent the night in a lovely hotel (get name) ...

In the morning, we found the local ire dealership (a massive operation -something tells me the owner doesn't want the road paved) and got a new tire. we looked around cachi (a nice enough spot, but not really worth hanging around= and were about to get on the road when we sideswiped another car. There followed a long attempt to sort things out, ....
