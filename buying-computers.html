--- layout: post title: Computer shopping advice ---

This is computer shopping advice for my aunt in law, but its reasonably general. It is advice for non-nerds - every nerd I know has his own opinions.

If you have money to spare: buy a mac. Laura has one. She got my old one, which lasted for about five years before having a water based accident. Apple did a good job at after sales support etc. My parents have one as well. apple.

If you don't have money to spare: Think about a Chromebook. I hate to be a shrill for my employer, but the Chromebook is a pretty good set-up. Advantages:

  • They are pretty cheap - about 200 quid.
  • In the event you break one, you don't lose any data.
  • They have a much safer security model, which means less spyware etc.

The disadvantage is that its solely a web browser - if you can't do it in the web now, you can't do it on a chrome book. I'm going to buy one and try it out when I get paid again. People have been singing the praises of The Samsung model.

Try out a tablet: Some people do all their stuff on a tablet. Good models are the ipad and the nexus 7. Supposed to be great for kids.

Avoid buying a desktop. Laptops take up less space, can be used from the sofa / the coffee shop / the bed, and have a battery which protects you from kicking the power plug out. There used to be two reasons against buying laptops: cost, and computing power. Cost has gone away, and power only matters today if you play new games or write code for a living.

Forget upgrading. Treat the computer as a closed box. On the one hand, upgrades are pretty easy if you know what you are doing. On the other, opening the case voids the warranty, and its still pretty easy to make a mistake and break the computer forever.

Avoid Windows: I find windows confusing and slow. Its also got tons of security problems. If you do end up with a windows computer, do take backups, run anti virus software, and so on.
