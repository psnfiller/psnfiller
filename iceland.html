We were in Iceland for a couple weeks. We rented a dodgy Vauxhall Astra, (online reviews discussed the trials and tribulations of a exhust falling off, but tat didn't happen to us), and drove around the Island. We camped, wich worked out well. We saw tons of wildlife, geolagy, and scenery. We ate fantastic donuts. Nowhere in the world does better donuts.


View Larger Map

We arrived in Reykjavík, and wandered around the city. One thing I noticed was that the city didn't look like it had been hit by the crash as much as Dublin or Madrid. We saw Harpa concert Hall, the Cathedral and the National museum. Its not a normal european capital with grand buildings. It felt much more like a fishing port with a side business, which I guess it is.

First stops were the Blue lagoon and Þingvellir National Park. The blue lagoon is perhaps the best flight stopover idea invented. Its a thremal spa not far from Keflick airport, the water is blue and warm and lovely. We didn't spend much time at Þingvellir, but its fantastic to look at the plates coming apart.

Camping worked out really well. Tons of places to camp, cheap campsites. We had worried that it would be too cold to camp, but it was nice and mild. The big problem was the sun heating up the tent in the morning.

We headed up to Húsavík in a fantastic drive past miles of snowy mountains. Jumped out of the car to peer at a Museum Reykholt about a saga author, Hraunfossar ( wikipedia says: Hraunfossar is a series of waterfalls formed by rivulets streaming over a distance of about 900 metres out of the Hallmundarhraun, a lava field which flowed from an eruption of one of the volcanoes lying under the glacier Langjökull.), Barnafoss and Deildartunguhver - water coming out of the ground at 97 degrees C and stinking of sulfur. We visited one of the northernmost Indian takeaways in the world in Akureyri. Oddly, Akureyri also has branches of investment banks - possibly its their punishment spot.

There's tons of birdlife. We were chasing Golden Plovers away as we put up the tent and were being woken up by drumming snipe. There's a Abundance of waders, geese and Eiders.

Only about 25 miles away from the Arctic Circle here. Húsavík is a fantastic place. Difficult to get a normal amount of sleep. Short walk, tired, lunch in bakery, Icelandic doughnuts are how doughnuts are supposed to be - very soft and light not stodgy or making you fell like you've eaten a brick 2 hours later. Whale museum and whale watching trip in the afternoon, traditional wooden boats, puffins (thousands) 5 or 6 humpback whales. Huge, up to 33m long, a baby could swim in their veins, heart the size of a VW. Whales feed in Iceland in the summer and breed in the south towards Panama. Whales are extremely intriguing with some species never having been seen in the wild.

We headed down to Lake Mývatn via Dettifoss and Goðafoss. Lake Mývatn is quite a touresty spot, but well worth it. Walked up Hverfell - a massive volcano crater. We went for a walk in Höfði nature park, and saw red-necked phalaropes!!. Around Skútustaðir, we saw s eudocraters, horned grebes, whooper swans, arctic terns and many others. We wandered around Hverir. Bubbling mud, steam, fumaroles, very colourful ground. We saw the geothremal power plant at Kofla and walked around the crater at the top of the hill - frozen. We swm in the hot pools while looking out of the lake. We didn't go to sleep until midnight - and it was still light.

We hung around Mývatn for as lng as we could, but in the end we had to get moving. The weather had been better in the north than the south, but we wanted to get around the island before having to go home. We pressed on, stopping at Dettifoss on the way. Europe's biggest waterfall is undescribable.

East Iceland is very wild. We saw reindeer by the road. The gravel road. This is the only road around the Island. We spent one evening crawling along at 30mph bating for the sun to set. We pressed on around the fjords into south Iceland. Here the landscape has been blasted by volcano and glacier - we drove for miles across glacier and volcano runoff, clouds above us, never seeing a soul.

Closer to Reykjavík, we started seeing signs of civilization again. As we came close enough to Reykjavík, bus loads of tourists appeared. Popular spots included Geysir, Seljalandsfoss and Gullfoss.
